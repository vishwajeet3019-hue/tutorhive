How to Improve Your Child's Maths Marks

Better maths marks usually come from three things: clearer concepts, regular practice, and less panic before tests.

Start by finding the real gap

A child may struggle with fractions because division is weak, or with word problems because they do not know how to translate language into steps. Before adding more worksheets, identify the missing concept.

Use short, regular practice

Twenty focused minutes several times a week is often more useful than one long cram session. Children improve faster when feedback arrives while the mistake is still fresh.

Choose support that explains, not only corrects

If your child needs more structure, explore online maths tuition that rebuilds concepts at the right pace and keeps parents informed.
