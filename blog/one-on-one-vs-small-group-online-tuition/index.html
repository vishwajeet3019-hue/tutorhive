1:1 vs Small Group Online Tuition: Which Is Better?

Both formats can work well. The right choice depends on your child's confidence, subject difficulty, learning pace, and need for personal attention.

1:1 Online Tuition

Best for personal attention

Choose 1:1 tuition when your child has concept gaps, feels shy asking questions, needs slower explanation, or is preparing for an important test. The class can fully follow your child's level and pace.

Small Group Tuition

Best for steady guided practice

Choose a small group when your child is comfortable with peers and needs structured revision. A group of up to 4 students keeps the class focused while still making learning interactive.

When should parents choose 1:1?

1:1 support is better when a student is behind in a subject, needs repeated explanation, or loses confidence quickly. It works especially well for maths problem solving, science concepts, English writing feedback, and exam doubt clearing.

When does small group tuition work well?

Small group tuition works when students are at a similar level and need regular practice. The group format can make lessons more energetic while still staying controlled and personal.
