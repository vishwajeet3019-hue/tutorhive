What Class 12 chemistry tuition covers

CBSE Class 12 chemistry has 16 chapters. Tuition is prioritised by board paper weightage.

Organic Chemistry — 28 marks

The highest-weightage section

  • Haloalkanes and Haloarenes — substitution and elimination
  • Alcohols, Phenols, and Ethers — reactions and distinctions
  • Aldehydes, Ketones, and Carboxylic Acids — nucleophilic addition
  • Amines — basicity, reactions, identification
Physical & Inorganic — 37 marks

Calculations, coordination, and d-block

  • Electrochemistry — cell potentials, Nernst equation, conductance
  • Chemical Kinetics — rate laws, order of reaction, Arrhenius equation
  • Coordination Compounds — IUPAC naming, isomerism, bonding theories
  • d and f Block Elements — properties and reactions of transition metals

Why organic chemistry determines Class 12 chemistry results

Organic chemistry carries 28 marks — the largest single section of the paper. Students who struggle here typically did not understand General Organic Chemistry (GOC) in Class 11. If that is the case, the first two to three sessions of Class 12 tuition are spent repairing that foundation before Class 12 organic chapters begin. This front-loaded investment pays back across all 5 organic chapters.

FAQs

Which chapters carry the most marks in Class 12 chemistry boards?

Organic Chemistry carries 28 marks and Physical Chemistry carries 23 marks. Together they account for over 60% of the board paper. Coordination Compounds (9 marks) is also highly learnable.

Why do students who understood Class 11 chemistry still struggle in Class 12?

Class 12 organic requires predicting products and writing mechanisms — not just naming compounds. Students who memorised Class 11 reactions without understanding GOC find Class 12 organic unpredictable.

How should a Class 12 chemistry board paper be approached?

Attempt Physical Chemistry first, then Inorganic, then Organic. Write reactions and mechanisms clearly — CBSE awards marks for each correct step, not just the final product.
